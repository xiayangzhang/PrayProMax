## 威卡 Gardnerian

In this place, in this hour, I stand between the worlds,  
with clean hands, clear speech, and a will held in Perfect Love and Perfect Trust.

I conjure thee, O Circle of Power,  
that thou beest a boundary between the world of men and the realms of the Mighty Ones,  
a guardian and a meeting-place for the working of health, wholeness, and peaceful longevity.

Lady of the Moon,  
Great Mother, Bright One, Queen of the secret place,  
hear the words spoken within this circle.

Whenever ye have need of anything,  
once in the month, and better it be when the moon is full,  
then shall ye assemble in some desert place,  
or in a forest all together join  
to adore the potent spirit of your queen.

Horned One,  
Lord of the wildwood, keeper of life and death,  
stand apart in thy own power and witness this working.

{{petition}}

Before the Lord I speak the boundary:  
This prayer seeks no taking from another’s life, no shortening of another’s days,  
no binding of any will, and no harm to any creature.  
Let only what is rightful, healing, and freely harmonious come to pass.

Let this prayer be for bodily health, freedom from sickness, and long peaceful well-being,  
harming none, binding none against the true will,  
and turning aside all falsehood, fear, and ill-wrought force.

Eko, Eko, Azarak,  
Eko, Eko, Zomelak.

By earth, let health take lawful root.  
By air, let breath and mind be clear.  
By fire, let the life-force be quickened without harm.  
By water, let healing flow in peace.

Lady of the Moon, receive this prayer.  
Horned One, witness this prayer.  
Mighty Ones of the circle, keep it within right measure.

As the cup and the bread are blessed in fellowship,  
so may the work be whole:  
for health, for the turning away of illness and pain,  
for blessed longevity and quiet strength,  
according to the law of harm to none.

May all that is spoken here remain clean, free, and rightly bounded.  
May no oathbound thing be touched, no secret name be forced,  
and no will be bent beneath this work.  
May this devotion stand in peace before the Lady and the Lord.

The circle is open, yet unbroken.  
Merry meet, and merry part, and merry meet again.
