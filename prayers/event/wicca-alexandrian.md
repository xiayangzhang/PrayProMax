## 威卡 Alexandrian

I stand within the Circle, in perfect love and perfect trust.  
Let all within this rite be bounded by the Rede:  
An it harm none, do what ye will.

By Air, by Fire, by Water, by Earth,  
this Circle is cast and sealed.  
Between the worlds, and in the presence of the Mighty Ones,  
let this rite be held for a clear, graceful, and fortunate job interview.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the East,  
powers of Air,  
I do summon, stir, and call you up,  
to witness and guard this rite.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the South,  
powers of Fire,  
I do summon, stir, and call you up,  
to witness and guard this rite.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the West,  
powers of Water,  
I do summon, stir, and call you up,  
to witness and guard this rite.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the North,  
powers of Earth,  
I do summon, stir, and call you up,  
to witness and guard this rite.

O Goddess,  
Great Mother, Lady of the Moon,  
Listen to the words of the Great Mother.  
Receive this prayer for calm mind, clear presence, and gracious speech.

O God,  
Lord of the Greenwood, Horned One,  
guardian of life, death, and return,  
Receive this prayer for courage, steadiness, and right opportunity.

Eko Eko Azarak,  
Eko Eko Zomelak,  
Eko Eko Azarak,  
Eko Eko Zomelak.

I seek not to dispossess another,  
nor to take by force what is not rightly mine;  
may the way open only where it is just, fitting, and free.

So mote it be.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the North,  
powers of Earth,  
I thank you for witnessing and guarding this rite.  
Hail and farewell.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the West,  
powers of Water,  
I thank you for witnessing and guarding this rite.  
Hail and farewell.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the South,  
powers of Fire,  
I thank you for witnessing and guarding this rite.  
Hail and farewell.

Ye Lords of the Watchtowers of the East,  
powers of Air,  
I thank you for witnessing and guarding this rite.  
Hail and farewell.

The Circle is open, but unbroken.  
Merry meet, merry part, and merry meet again.

Blessed be.

If any word has overreached, let it fall away.  
If any desire would bind another will, let it be released.  
By Earth beneath, by breath within, I am grounded and at peace.  
An it harm none, do what ye will.  
So mote it be.
