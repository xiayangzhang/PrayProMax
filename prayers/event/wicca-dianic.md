## 威卡 Dianic

By earth and moon, by breath and flame,  
this place is made sacred.

I cast this circle as a boundary of truth,  
a circle of protection,  
a circle of blessing,  
a circle of the Goddess.

O Diana,  
Bright Lady of the Moon,  
Mother of the wild places,  
Keeper of women’s mysteries,  
be present in this circle.

{{petition}}

Let this prayer touch only what may be touched in freedom.  
Let it bind no unwilling heart.  
Let it cross no oath-bound door.  
Let it harm none.

O Diana,  
look upon this path of seeking work,  
and upon the meeting, the words, the choices, and the outcome.

Where there is fear, let there be clear boundary.  
Where there is confusion, let there be inner knowing.  
Where there is haste, let there be centered breath.  
Where there is self-doubt, let there be quiet strength.  
Where there is opportunity, let it open rightly.  
Where there is recognition, let it be freely given.  
Where there is a fitting place, let the way become clear.

All acts of love and pleasure are my rituals.

I place within this circle  
my attention, my breath, my honest preparation,  
and this prayer offered without bargain.

By the waxing, full, and waning Moon,  
by the dark and returning Moon,  
may what is true be strengthened,  
may what is harmful be dissolved,  
may what belongs to me return to me,  
may what is not mine depart in peace.

For a smooth interview, clear presence, natural speech,  
fair appreciation, and a fitting opportunity,  
I ask blessing, clarity, and right unfolding.

I seek no place by dispossessing another.  
I seek no success by diminishing another’s life, luck, dignity, or freedom.  
May all rightful paths remain open to all.

An it harm none, do what ye will.

O Diana,  
Bright Lady of the Moon,  
I thank you for your presence.  
Cleanse this circle of grasping, fear, and compulsion.  
Let only free blessing remain.  
Let every will be unbound,  
every choice remain its own,  
and every outcome unfold in truth.

Remain if you will,  
depart if you must,  
with blessing between us.

The circle is open,  
but unbroken.  
Merry meet,  
merry part,  
and merry meet again.
