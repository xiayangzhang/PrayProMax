## 威卡 Dianic

By earth and moon, by breath and flame,  
this place is made sacred.

I cast this circle as a boundary of truth,  
a circle of protection,  
a circle of blessing,  
a circle of the Goddess.

O Diana,  
Bright Lady of the Moon,  
Mother of the wild places,  
Keeper of women’s mysteries,  
be present in this circle.

{{petition}}

Let this prayer touch only what may be touched in freedom.  
Let it bind no unwilling heart.  
Let it cross no oath-bound door.  
Let it harm none.

O Diana,  
look upon the departed ones with mercy and moonlit grace.

Where there is fear, let there be quiet.  
Where there is pain, let there be release.  
Where there is wandering, let there be a clear path.  
Where there is sorrow, let there be remembrance.  
Where there is unfinished grief, let there be peace.  
Where there is love, let it remain free.

Receive them, if it be right,  
into the deep womb of the Goddess,  
into the Summerland of rest,  
into the silver silence where suffering is dissolved  
and the soul is held in blessing.

All acts of love and pleasure are my rituals.

I place within this circle:  
memory, gratitude, tears, and love,  
given freely, without bargain and without demand.

By the waxing, full, and waning Moon,  
by the dark and returning Moon,  
may what is true be strengthened,  
may what is harmful be dissolved,  
may the dead be at peace,  
may the living be comforted,  
may all that is bound in grief be gently released.

For the peace of the ancestors and beloved dead,  
I ask blessing, clarity, and right unfolding.

An it harm none, do what ye will.

O Diana,  
I thank you for your presence.  
Remain if you will,  
depart if you must,  
with blessing between us.

The circle is open,  
but unbroken.  
Merry meet,  
merry part,  
and merry meet again.

By earth and moon, by breath and flame,  
let every word of this rite be cleansed of grasping,  
sealed in freedom,  
guarded by clear boundaries,  
and returned only as blessing.
