## 长屋宗教（Longhouse / Handsome Lake）

Shé:kon.  
Skennen’kó:wa ken?

Ohén:ton Karihwatéhkwen.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne Onkwehón:we.  
We give thanks for the People, for families, neighbors, honest speech, steady hands, and the Good Mind that keeps work and household in balance.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne Yethi’nistenha Ohwéntsia.  
We give thanks to Mother Earth, who carries the home, the fields, the paths of labor, and the places where food and livelihood are made.  
May the house be full in a rightful way, with enough to share, enough to repair what is worn, and enough to keep peace among those who dwell there.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne kahnekaronnyòn:ke.  
We give thanks to the Waters, that cleanse, sustain, and keep life moving; may our work flow cleanly, without deceit, waste, or harm.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne kontírio.  
Nia:wen kówa ne ononhkwa’shón:’a.  
Nia:wen kówa ne ohonte’shón:’a.  
We give thanks for the fish, the medicines, the plants, and the food plants, especially the sustaining foods that teach patience, planting, tending, and harvest.  
May prosperity come as a good harvest comes: through right care, clear seasons, faithful labor, and gratitude.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne otsí:nonwa.  
Nia:wen kówa ne kentsyon’shón:’a.  
Nia:wen kówa ne rotiwé:ras.  
We give thanks for the animals, the trees, and the birds, who remind us that wealth is not greed, but right relation, shelter, food, skill, and generosity.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne karhákon:ha.  
Nia:wen kówa ne yotkaráhkwa.  
We give thanks for the Four Winds and the Thunderers, who move the air and bring renewal.  
May obstacles before honest work be moved aside without bringing suffering to others.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne iethihsótha áhsonthenhne.  
Nia:wen kówa ne otsistohkwa’shón:’a.  
We give thanks for the Sun, Grandmother Moon, and the Stars, who mark the days, seasons, rest, and renewal.  
May our plans be timely, our judgment steady, our trade fair, and our household well ordered.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa ne Shonkwaia’tíson.  
We give thanks to the Creator, and we remember the teachings that call people away from drunkenness, lying, violence, gambling sickness, abandonment of kin, and all conduct that breaks the peace of the home.  
May wealth come through a clear mind, honest labor, mutual care, and good purpose.  
We do not ask to take another person’s portion, to shorten another life, or to make any household empty; we ask only for sufficiency, right increase, and the strength to share what is good.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.

Nia:wen kówa.  
We close by setting our words down in thankfulness, staying within the open words of thanksgiving, honoring the Creator and all our relations without overreaching, without barter, without harm, and without pretending to what is not ours.  
Étho niiohtónhak ne onkwa’nikòn:ra.
