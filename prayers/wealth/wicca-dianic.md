## 威卡 Dianic

By earth and moon, by breath and flame,  
this place is made sacred.

I cast this circle as a boundary of truth,  
a circle of protection,  
a circle of blessing,  
a circle of the Goddess.

O Diana,  
Bright Lady of the Moon,  
Mother of the wild places,  
Keeper of women’s mysteries,  
be present in this circle.

{{petition}}

Let this prayer touch only what may be touched in freedom.  
Let it bind no unwilling heart.  
Let it cross no oath-bound door.  
Let it harm none.

O Diana,  
look upon this livelihood, this labor, and this household.

Where there is scarcity, let there be wise increase.  
Where there is confusion, let there be clear discernment.  
Where there is obstruction, let the way open without harm.  
Where there is waste, let there be stewardship.  
Where there is fear, let there be grounded courage.  
Where there is honest effort, let it be fruitful.

May prosperity come without theft, coercion, or dispossession.  
May no one be deprived for this abundance to grow.  
May no life be shortened, no blessing stolen, no household diminished.  
Let gain arise through right opportunity, fair exchange, skillful work, and generous flow.

All acts of love and pleasure are my rituals.

I place within this circle gratitude for bread, cup, hearth, labor, and moonlit blessing.

By the waxing, full, and waning Moon,  
by the dark and returning Moon,  
may what is true be strengthened,  
may what is harmful be dissolved,  
may what belongs here return in peace,  
may what is not ours depart in peace.

For broadening resources, smooth endeavor, and a flourishing home,  
I ask blessing, clarity, and right unfolding.

An it harm none, do what ye will.

O Diana,  
I thank you for your presence.  
Remain if you will,  
depart if you must,  
with blessing between us.

May this circle be cleansed of grasping, envy, and fear.  
May all power raised here return to the earth as blessing.  
May every wish be bounded by freedom, consent, and harmlessness.  
May no oath be broken, no will be forced, no hidden door be crossed.  
In peace, in gratitude, in the Lady’s light, this rite is released.

The circle is open,  
but unbroken.  
Merry meet,  
merry part,  
and merry meet again.
